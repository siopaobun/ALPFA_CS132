Is there going to be enough food to feed all Filipinos?


In Decmber 2023, the number of Filipino families that expereinced hunger was at 12.6%. That's about ,325,632 families going hungry, not being able to eat at least once in the past three months. So that begs the question, how are we coping up as a country food wise? With a steadily increasing population currently at 119 million, the Philippines grows at an average pace of 1,700,000 people each year. With that being said, is there going to be enough food to be put on everyone's tables?

Our project aims to shed some light on these alarming concerns. By focusing primarly on the growth of internal food production of the country, we can gain some insight on the relationship between the Philippine’s growing population and the overall sustainability of the country food wise. Moreover, our project also includes supplements for our findings with data related to food imports, as well as annual hunger indexes to help concretely evaluate the country’s performance in both the economic sector and the health sector as well.

Conclusively, we also aim to provide a bold prediction: If the Phillipines, with our current growth in food production, will ever (and continue to) be able to keep up with its growing population.

Problem

Is the Philippines' food industry production keeping up with its population growth?

Solution

Our solution is to use data science to gain insights on the relationship between local food production and local population growth and foresee potential problems.


This leads us to ask,

Is the Philippines' food industry production keeping up with its population growth?

Will the country eventually experience a sustainable relationship between the country's population growth and food production?

Null Hypothesis

The rate of food production is at a steady pace with respect to the population growth.

Alternative Hypothesis

The population growth significantly outpaces the rate of food production.

To assess and evaluate the Philippines' Performance in terms of Internal Food Production vs Population Growth Ratio.

We explored the internet to gather relevant data,

and here's what we found:

In the process of finding relevant datasets, the team went to different libraries within UP Diliman. Ultimately, we were able to find most assistance from the School of Economics Library. After signing necessary paperwork, the library staff gave us access to several websites than we can use. Among the provided websites, we ended up using CDMNext, the next generation of CEIC Data manager, which allows us to access CEIC databases. This is where we found our dataset regarding the industry production index, specifically in food products. Furthermore, we found the dataset for the Philippines' population growth in macrotrends.net, a public global metrics website.
